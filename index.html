ch1

Trixie and Katya arrive at the woods, they’re vast and dark, they’re one of the only parts of town that haven’t been developed and it’s a foreboding presence in front of them. Katya turns the car off, their surroundings becoming even more ominous without the illumination of the headlights. The air around them is still, the trees ahead looking like a scary movie set with the way they are completely still. 

Katya grabs her backpack from the backseat where she kept it protected during the drive. She slams the door closed and slings it over her shoulder, noticing that Trixie is still sitting still in the front seat, not seeming to want to exit the safety of the car. She walks around to the passenger side of the car, and opens the front door, “you ready babe?” she asks, encouraging Trixie to leave the warmth of the vehicle. 

Trixie looks at her, fear evident in her eyes, “Katya, I’m not so sure about this,” her voice is wavering, and Katya kind of feels bad for dragging her out to do this. 

“Come on,” Katya whines, “I’ll hold your hand the whole time, I promise,” she adds, softer, hoping it will tempt Trixie to leave the car. 

“It’s pitch black out there, Kat,” Trixie points out. 

“That’s why I brought,” Katya reaches behind her blindly, feeling around for the flashlights she knows she packed in her bag, she eventually finds them, bringing them around to show Trixie triumphantly, “these!” 

Trixie takes one with a shaky hand, inspecting it for a moment before turning it on, clearly checking to see if Katya remembered batteries. She still makes no move to exit the car, she’s rolling the flashlight between her hands nervously, her eyes drifting off toward the trees, as if she’s waiting for a sign that it’s safe to venture in. 

Katya waits patiently, but Trixie makes no moves to stand up. She sighs, “we don’t have to do this, we can just go home,” she suggests, not wanting to force her girlfriend to do something she so clearly doesn’t want to do. 

Trixie looks up at Katya, seeing the disappointment on her features and sucks in a deep breath, summons all of her courage, and schools her features, determined to go ghost hunting with her girlfriend, “no, let’s go,” she says decidedly. 

Katya smiles brightly, but Trixie still makes no move to exit the car. Katya reaches her hand out to her, and Trixie takes it, holding it tightly as she finally stands up and closes the car door. Katya kisses her softly when she turns back around, smiling comfortingly at the taller woman and they stand there for a second, Katya vibrating in anticipation and Trixie trying to focus on not being terrified. 

Katya makes the first move, pulling Trixie along through the small dirt parking lot at the edge of the tree line. They approach the trees, there’s an entrance where the trail that runs through the forest is, a path that they’ve walked together many times. 

Katya stops them at the beginning of the path, listening to the way Trixie’s breathing is faster than normal, giving her hand an encouraging squeeze. “Should we follow the path, or find our own way,” she asks. 

Trixie looks around, flashing her flashlight all around them to try to find the direction that seems the least intimidating. “Both options sound awful,” she finally responds after she determines that they are surrounded by absolutely nothing but miles and miles of crushing darkness. 




ch2

They decide to follow the path, walking down it hand in hand. It gets colder the further they go into the woods, and Trixie is grateful for the walking furnace her hand is attached to. As they walk down the path, Trixie keeps her flashlight moving at a constant pace around them, making sure to look in every direction so that nothing sneaks up on them. The grip she has on Katya’s hand is crushing and Katya is starting to lose feeling on her fingers, but she doesn’t complain. 

Trixie jumps at every little noise they hear and Katya reassures her every time that they’re okay, whispering comfortingly to her. They walk for a while, Katya stopping them every once and a while to stand still for a few minutes to, “let the ghosts catch up.” Trixie giggles at that a little, Katya’s fascination with ghosts is adorable, despite the fact that it drags Trixie into the middle of the woods on Halloween night. 

After about fifteen minutes of walking along the path that Trixie doesn’t remember ever being quite this long, something starts to change. At first, they don’t really notice it, continuing to walk, but the more they walk the more apparent it becomes that the air around them has grown foggy. It’s becoming harder to see ahead of them, Trixie’s flashlight isn’t allowing her to see very deep into the woods anymore, and it’s making her panic. 

“Katya what’s happening,” her voice wavers as she says it, her heart rate spiking so Katya can feel it in her wrist that’s pressed tightly to hers. 

“It’s okay, baby it’s just some fog,” she tries to sound reassuring, but her voice breaks slightly in fear. 

“I can’t see where we’re going,” Trixie panics, not liking that she can’t be sure that they aren’t being followed, she can’t help but feel paranoid. 

“Come on, we have to keep walking,” Katya says, pulling her hand to get her to walk again, they need to continue on the path to make it out of the fog or the woods. 

They continue walking, Trixie’s footsteps are both hesitant and urgent, eager to get out of the fog so she can see again, but scared to continue down the path when she can’t see it. 

Katya stops moving again, Trixie bumping into her from behind, “why’d you stop,” she hisses, nudging Katya to walk again so they can get the hell out of the woods. 

“Trix look,” she says, tugging on her arm to get her to see whatever she’s looking at. 

Trixie looks over Katya’s shoulder, holding her flashlight out to get a better look, she has to squint her eyes to see through the fog, but she realizes that Katya stopped because the path separates into two directions. Trixie tries to think back to the other times they’ve been on the path, not remembering there being more than one way to go. She tries not to let herself think too much about it so she doesn’t get any more scared than she already is. 

“Which way do we go,” Katya asks, Trixie envies how even her voice is, how calm she’s managing to stay when Trixie is sure she’s never been more scared in her life. 

Trixie is about to answer when she hears a noise from deep in the woods. This is definitely the most scared she’s ever been in her life. The noise is an odd sort of ringing, it echoes through the trees, and it sounds both far away and nearby, it’s unlike anything either of them has ever heard before. Trixie is trembling now, Katya squeezing her hand and stepping closer so their bodies are touching, trying to calm her terrified girlfriend down a little. 

“What was that?” Trixie whimpers. 

“I don’t know,” Katya whispers back, “should we go see?” 

Trixie looks at her as if she’s gone crazy, maybe she has, but pauses to think. The noise might be another person who could give them directions out of the woods that she thinks they might be lost in, but it could also be another person that wants to hurt them. 



ch3

“Come on, let's go this way,” Katya says, veering off to the left, cutting through the trees. 

It’s darker here than it was on the path, at least there the sky was partially exposed along the walkway. They’re barely a hundred feet from the path, but Trixie can’t even see it when she turns around. The details of the woods are completely swallowed by darkness, the moon blocked from view. 

The flashlights only marginally help and Trixie refuses to look anywhere but where the light beam is illuminating after the third time she sees a menacing face staring at her in the darkness. 

“Katya I don’t like this,” Trixie says, voice shaking against her will. 

“It’s okay, I got you,” Katya says, stepping closer to Trixie so their arms brush against each other as they walk. 

“There’s nothing here except bugs and probably bears,” Trixie complains. 

Katya’s in the middle of responding, trying to crack a joke to loosen the tension and make Trixie less scared when she stops speaking, stopping in her tracks entirely. 

“Babe, look,” Katya says in awe pointing to a spot ahead of them. 

Trixie turns to look, seeing a glowing light coming from deep in the woods. It has a bluish hue to it, clearly not coming from either of their flashlights, and she feels the hair on her arms sticking up. 

“What is that?” Trixie asks, dumbfounded. 

“I don’t know!” Katya says. She sounds way too excited to see an unknown glow coming from the woods that they are currently, more or less, trapped in. 

“Don’t sound so excited,” Trixie says pointedly, “it could be dangerous.” 

“What if it’s a ghost!” Katya exclaims, bouncing on her heels at the thought, “can we go see? Please?” 

“I don’t know,” Trixie says, hating the way it makes Katya deflate, but she’s the cautious one, the one keeping them safe. Relatively. 

ch4

“Let’s follow the noise, maybe whoever it is can help us,” Trixie decides, more willing to go after hope than to wander off back into unknown territory. 

Katya nods, squeezing Trixie’s hand again and planting another soft kiss on her cheek before she starts pulling her off in the direction they heard the noise in. They walk down the path aimlessly, trying to stay quiet to hear any more noises that might occur. The fog gradually starts to lift around them and they can see a little better now, and a wave of relief washes over Trixie. 

They continue walking, going deeper and deeper and Trixie starts to grow nervous again, they’re venturing into unfamiliar parts of the woods, and she’s lost track of what direction they came from, and they haven’t heard another noise since the first one. Her heart rate goes up again and just when she thinks they’re definitely lost, the noise starts again. The mysterious ringing sounds closer this time, though they can’t tell how they know that it sounds like it’s coming from everywhere. 

They keep walking, the noise is more frequent now, and they assume they’re going in the right direction. The noise eventually cuts off and Trixie worries that their hope is lost. Then, there’s a soft glow emanating from the trees up ahead of them. 

They make eye contact briefly, asking with their eyes if they should see what the glowing is. They seem to decide that they should see what it is, moving ahead with certainty but also caution. Trixie’s death grip on Katya’s hand still hasn’t let up and Katya fears that she might end this night with a broken finger or two. 

They walk slowly forward, careful with their steps in case whatever lies ahead is something that shouldn’t be alerted of their presence. Trixie tries to calm her breathing down, holding her breath the closer they get to the strange lights. 

They make it closer to the lights, hiding behind a tree and poking their heads around to look at what is causing the glow. About ten feet ahead of them is a large ball of blue light, hovering above the ground and gently floating as if it’s riding the waves of the ocean. It doesn’t seem dangerous or threatening, though it is definitely strange. 

They turn to face each other again, Trixie with a look of utter confusion and Katya with one of pure delight and excitement. “What is that,” Trixie asks, hoping Katya has a better understanding of what is going on than her. 

“Trixie,” she says back, bouncing up and down in her excitement, “Trixie,” she says again unhelpfully. 

“What, what is it Kat,” Trixie is slightly exasperated, though she is slightly calmer seeing that Katya isn’t scared. 

“I think it’s a will-o-the-wisp,” Katya says, her voice rising in volume. 

“A will-o-what?” Trixie questions. 

“A will-o-the-wisp,” Katya repeats back. 

“Yeah, I heard you, what the fuck is that?” Trixie snaps back, losing her patience. 

“They’re ghost lights, they’re supposed to lead you to something,” Katya explains. 

“What do they lead you to?” Trixie asks, needing to be sure they’re not going to follow something dangerous. 

“It could be anything,” she answers back, not giving Trixie much hope that this is a good idea, “I think we should follow them.” 

Trixie thinks it over, unsure about whether or not this is a good idea, “I’m not sure, Kat.” 

“Come on,” Katya whines, “we found something supernatural, we can’t give up now!” 

Trixie sighs, she doesn’t want to disappoint the clearly excited blonde beside her, but she also doesn’t want to put them in any kind of dangerous or questionable situation. 



“I don’t think we should, Kat,” Trixie says. She doesn’t want to disappoint her but this does not feel like a smart idea. The light looks too unnatural and she is not about to die because she willingly walked toward an unnatural light in the woods. 

“Okay,” Katya says, she’s slightly disappointed, but she gets to keep exploring now and that’s just as much fun. 

She tugs Trixie’s arm again, leading them away from the light and deeper into the dark woods. Trixie is starting to regret her decision, at least if they would have gone toward the light she would’ve been able to see better. 

They walk for some time, the light behind them now, drowned out by the sea of trees around them and long forgotten. 

Katya is having a great time, trotting along and looking around curiously, no care or fear in the world. Trixie on the other hand is still on edge, only turning to look when she hears a noise and has to whip her flashlight around to make sure it’s not an axe murderer. She regrets being a horror fan for the first time for how paranoid it has her. 

“Look, Trixie!” Katya says again, once more bouncing on her heels. 

It’s so similar to how she reacted earlier that when Trixie looks up and sees another light in front of them she questions if they’re stuck in a very short time loop. 

“This again,” she says, almost under her breath but loud enough for Katya to hear and snort at. 

“Come on, it’s a sign we have to find out what it is,” she says.

They walk slightly closer and Trixie notices that this light is different from the last one. It’s not as steady, it flickers slightly and it isn’t the same cold, blue tone as the other light they saw, this one is warmer, a glow rather than illumination. 

“I still don’t think it’s a good idea,” Trixie says. 

“Come on, we ignored the first one but this time it’s too big of a coincidence we need to explore,” Katya whines.

Trixie sighs, hoping she isn’t making the wrong decision for the millionth time that night, “let's follow them.” 

Katya jumps up and down, leaving a sloppy kiss on Trixie’s cheek in her excitement, “thank you!” 

Trixie tugs on her hand gently to get her to calm back down, kissing her softly, trying to absorb some of the confidence and calm that Katya has in this situation. 

Katya starts walking toward the glowing orb, and Trixie grows worried again, not sure that it’s a good idea to follow a mysterious blue glowing orb through the woods that aren’t familiar in the way they should be. As they get closer to the floating light, Trixie can feel an odd heat warm her face as if the thing is radiating warm energy. Before they are within touching distance of the apparition, it moves with an odd movement. It zips through the woods, further in the distance but in a place they can still see it, yet they can see a faint glowing trail left behind it, illuminating the path they’re meant to take. 

They follow behind the glowing orbs for what feels like forever, going deeper and deeper into the woods, their surroundings becoming even more unfamiliar. Anticipation is building the further they walk, both anxious to see what they are being led toward. 

It’s almost as if they can feel it when they arrive at their destination, it feels like they are in a place that’s safe, familiar, even though they are sure they have never been here before. 

“I think we’re here,” Katya whispers, echoing Trixie’s thoughts. 

Right after she says it, the wisp glows brighter than before, growing slightly in size before it disappears with a flash, leaving a ringing in its wake. Suddenly, it’s dark again, leaving their eyes struggling to adjust to the sudden darkness. Trixie hadn’t even realized that she’d turned her flashlight off as they followed the path set out for them, and she turns it back on, trying to figure out where they were led. 

“Where are we?” she asks, hoping Katya can make sense of where they are. 

Katya is looking around too, inspecting their surroundings, until the beam of her flashlight falls upon something gray and stone. It wouldn’t seem odd to her, but the shape of it is strange for a reason she can’t place. She walks over to it, at some point her and Trixie’s hands had let go, both of them feeling safe following the will-o-the-wisp trail. She leaves Trixie behind as she goes to inspect the stone, and when she makes her way to it, she has to clear the leaves and debris that are surrounding it, making it hard to see in its entirety. 

She cleans it off, pointing her flashlight to it and she realizes that it’s a headstone. 

“Trixie,” she calls out to her girlfriend, “come look at this.” 

Trixie walks over, there’s less caution in her steps than before, and stands next to Katya, looking down at the headstone, “are we in a graveyard?” she asks. 

“I don’t think so,” Katya responds, “it looks like this is the only grave.” 

Trixie looks around, trying to find another headstone to make sense of this, but Katya is right, this is the only headstone in the surrounding area. Trixie takes Katya’s hand again, feeling uneasy again. 

“Why did it lead us here?” Trixie asks, fear slipping into her voice again. 

“I don’t know,” Katya whispers, trying to read the inscription on the stone, but it’s not in English or any other language that she knows. Despite the oddness of this entire situation, Katya doesn’t feel scared, she feels at ease here. 

“What are we supposed to do?” Trixie continues her line of questioning. 

“Maybe we’re just supposed to wait and see what happens?” Katya suggests, unsure of herself.

Trixie shrugs, she doesn’t have any better ideas, though she isn’t entirely comfortable standing around in the middle of these woods by a gravesite. 

“Oh!” Katya exclaims, “I also brought a ouija board, we could try contacting whoever this is?” she suggests. 

“You brought a ouija board?” Trixie asks incredulously. 

“Of course I did,” Katya sounds mildly offended, “what kind of ghost hunter doesn’t bring a ouija board with them?” 

“Of course, my bad,” Trixie deadpans. 

“Well, what should we do?” Katya asks.

They walk and walk, getting deeper and deeper into the woods until their legs are aching with exhaustion, but they find nothing.

As they continue to wait, Trixie gets more and more impatient, willing anything to happen, even something insignificant, just so they can leave. She feels fatigue washing over her from the combination of walking for what feels like hours, fear, and adrenaline, and her body starts to slouch, leaning up against Katya who is waiting patiently, at attention. 

Just when she rests her head on Katya’s shoulder and her eyes start to flutter closed in her exhaustion, she feels Katya perk up underneath her. Katya grabs her arm, “Trixie,” she whispers loudly, “Trixie wake up,” she whispers, shaking Trixie to get her to pay attention to whatever has caught her attention. 

Trixie lifts her head from the warmth of her girlfriend’s neck, rubbing at her tired eyes and trying to get them to focus back on the world around them, “what is it?” 

A gust of wind sweeps through the forest with a surprising force, making them both shiver before it settles back down, shifting into a more gentle breeze. 

Trixie’s eyes are finally working again and she follows Katya’s gaze, her eyes landing on what appears to be a human-like figure glowing a dull silvery gray. Her eyes widen at the sight, her jaw going slack as she stares at what can’t be anything other than a ghost. Katya’s grip on her arm gets tighter as they both stare at the ghost, its back turned to them so they aren’t yet seen. 

“Is that,” Trixie trails off, not sure if she wants to make what she’s seeing real by saying it out loud. 

“It’s a ghost!” Katya exclaims, shaking Trixie’s arm now, “we found a ghost! They’re real!” her voice is getting louder and Trixie shushes her, doesn’t want to gain the attention of the ghost in case it’s dangerous. 

“Trixie we found a ghost!” she whisper-screams again, unable to contain herself. 

“Apparently,” Trixie responds, sounding unsure. A part of her wonders if she actually did fall asleep on Katya’s shoulder and she’s just dreaming, but something tells her that she’s awake, it all feels too real to be a dream. 

“What should we do?” Katya whispers. 

“I don’t know,” Trixie whispers back, “you’re the ghost expert, you tell me.” 

“Do you think we should go try to talk to it?” Katya asks, “or just wait and see what it does?” Trixie doesn’t know if she’s asking her or just thinking out loud and gives her a minute to figure it out.

Katya rifles through her bag for a second before pulling out the ouija board and she unzips the front pocket to grab the planchette. She sets both down on the ground, careful not to put it on top of any specific grave, and sits down on the ground, patting the empty space beside her for Trixie to sit too. 

Trixie sits reluctantly, a chill going up her spine when she hits the cold ground. She has her back to the woods behind her and it’s an eerie feeling, she can’t shake the feeling that there’s something in there, lurking behind her. 

“Okay,” Katya says, placing the planchette on the board delicately, “your fingers please.” 

“That’s what she said,” Trixie responds, moving her fingers onto the planchette anyway. 

“This is a serious matter, Tracy, this is no time for jokes.” Katya responds, humor in her voice despite the words. 

“Sorry,” Trixie responds, not sorry at all. 

Katya places her fingers on the planchette across from Trixie’s, looking up at her, “ready?” 

Trixie nods, her stomach feels like it’s in a loose knot, she doesn’t believe in ouija boards but there’s something about doing it in a graveyard that has her spooked. 

Katya starts moving the piece in figure eights around the board, making sure they’re holding it loosely enough for the planchette to move, and then she speaks, “is there anyone here that wants to talk to us?” 

They both fall silent, sitting still, staring at their hands waiting for something to happen. They wait for a couple of minutes, and just when they think nothing is going to happen, their fingers start moving. 

“Trixie look!” Katya whispers excitedly. 

The planchette moves somewhat slowly, drifting across the board and landing firmly over yes.

“Did you do that?” Trixie whispers, it sounds like something Katya would do. 

“No!” Katya defends quickly, “I swear I wouldn’t do something like that, you know how serious I am about ghosts.” 

She makes a good point, but Trixie still isn’t sure she trusts her. There’s no way a ghost is communicating to them through this children’s game. 

“Are you a demon?” Katya questions into the open air. 

“Why would they say yes if they are,” Trixie questions. 

“Don’t lie,” Katya yells to the air. 

The planchette starts moving again, still pretty slowly. It moves across the board, landing on no this time. 

Katya smiles up at Trixie and she rolls her eyes. 

“How old are you?” Katya asks this time. 

The piece moves once again and it seems to be moving slightly faster, as if the ghost is gaining confidence. It moves over the 1 first, stopping briefly before moving to the 3. 

“13,” Katya whispers, “that’s so young.” 

They’re quiet for a moment, taking in the information before speaking again. They ask a few more questions, Trixie joining in shortly after when she starts feeling more comfortable. They learn that the ghost is named Russell and basic facts about his life. 

“How did you die?” Katya asks. 

“Katya! You can’t just ask a kid that,” Trixie scolds her. 

“Sorry,” Katya says, “you don’t have to answer that.” 

Their hands are still suddenly, no longer moving consistently around the board. 

“Are you still there?” Katya questions after a couple minutes of silence. 

The planchette moves back to the yes and Katya exhales in a sigh of relief. The piece starts moving again, to letters, spelling out L-I-O-N. 

“Lion?” Katya questions out loud, “you got killed by a lion?</i> her jaw falls open slightly in shock. This kid is cooler than she will ever be.

“You motherfucker,” Katya says as Trixie cackles loudly. 

They spend hours talking to Russell, learning all about his life, his interests, his family. Just when the sun starts rising, Russell moves the planchette again, hesitating before moving to goodbye.Trixie feels a pang in her heart thinking about Russell, only 13, alone in these woods. She feels guilty having to leave him behind, likely never to talk to him again. She wonders if any of his family members are still out there, he spoke to them as though he died recently, she wonders if she could tell someone that she found him and he’s okay. 

Katya looks like she’s feeling similar, sadness in her eyes as she swallows thickly, “bye Russell,” she whispers and the planchette goes still. 

They take their hands off the board, looking at each other, not knowing what to say. In a while they’ll get up, heading back through the woods in silence to the car and drive home. The morning will bring a normal day, and Russell will be pushed to the back of their minds, never gone completely, a constant presence over their lives, a living memory to keep him alive.

Trixie looks over at her. Katya still seems unsure, for the first time since they got here showing uncertainty. Katya makes no moves to get up, so Trixie rises, pulls Katya’s hand up with her to get her to stand too. “Alright, let’s go,” Trixie decides, taking on the role of the courageous one now. 

Katya nods her head, following Trixie’s lead and they make their way slowly over to where the ghost is floating. As they get closer, it seems as though the ghost is looking for something, floating around slowly and turning its head to look around. 

Before they manage to reach the ghost to talk to it, a stick breaks beneath one of their feet, a loud snapping noise sounding out, disturbing the complete silence of the woods that hung around them until that moment. They both freeze, not sure what to make of what just happened, and watch as the ghost whips around to face them, unhinging its jaw at an alarming speed and letting out an ear-piercing noise that Trixie thinks is a scream. 

They both stand frozen on their spots, not sure what to do. The ghost remains in front of them, its mouth open wider than a human’s mouth could be, and bellowing a scream from deep within it, the sound is awful and gut-wrenching, and neither one knows what to do.

They decide to watch the ghost, observing the way it seems to be floating aimlessly but with some sort of intent. Katya’s mouth is hanging open, her eyes shining with wonder as she watches a real-life ghost, just like she always wanted. 

They stand together, at a distance away from the ghost in order to be able to watch it quietly. They manage to go unnoticed for quite a few minutes, not learning much about the ghost other than it is clearly looking for something. The ghost is slowly making its way around the area, turning in circles, and then suddenly it’s facing them, its eyes meeting their figures and time feels like it slows down in the few seconds it takes for the ghost to unhinge its ghost jaw and let out a deep scream, unlike anything they had ever heard. It’s ear piercing in both volume and tone and it sounds somehow so human in its emotion but so inhuman in its sound. 

Their hands are up, covering their ears before they even realize they’re doing it, and though it doesn’t help much in making things quieter, it does stop the way it feels like it’s entering their ears, coursing through their bodies. 

They make eye contact, locking eyes and trying to communicate silently and Katya is glad to see that Trixie isn’t panicking as much as she thought she would be. They wait there for what feels like hours, covering their ears and trying to drown out the noise that is seeping into their skin. 

Eventually, the ghost stops screaming. When they deem it safe, they both slowly remove the hands from their ears. Katya takes Trixie’s hand back in hers, asking if she’s alright as quietly as she can and Trixie nods, not willing to speak and cause the ghost to scream again. 

The ghost is staring at them now, looking unsure and Trixie almost laughs at how the ghost seems more scared of them than she is of it. 

“Um, hi,” Katya speaks loud enough for the ghost to hear, testing the waters. 

The ghost’s eyes widen and it floats a little closer to them. 

“Hi,” it responds, and its voice is different than Trixie expected, it’s not scary, it sounds human but with a weird metal quality to it, like the voice is being filtered through a metal tube. 

The ghost is still staring at them, and they’re both staring right back, at a stalemate with how to continue. 

“Who are you?” the ghost breaks the silence. 

Now that it’s not screaming at them anymore, Trixie is able to get a much better look. She realizes that it’s not an it it’s a she, at least she thinks she is with the long hair that’s tied in a braid that’s semi apparent through her translucent body, and the flowing gown that’s billowing around her. 

“I’m Katya,” Katya introduces herself with her usual relentless enthusiasm, “and this is Trixie,” she motions to Trixie with the hand that isn’t holding Trixie’s own.

“I’m sorry I yelled,” the ghost says quietly, she looks like she shrinks in size too. 

“It’s okay,” Katya says, not completely sure how to go about comforting a ghost, “are you alright?” 

The ghost looks up at that, stares straight through Katya and it makes Trixie recoil.
